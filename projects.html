Budget Facility for Infrastructure

Programme secretarial and assessment of project proposals totalling R153 billion for National Treasury

Client: National Treasury
Duration: 2018-2022
Location: South Africa (National)
Sector: Financial Advisory

Project Overview

The Budget Facility for Infrastructure (BFI) is a critical initiative by the National Treasury to assess and prioritize large-scale infrastructure projects that require government funding. bd&company was appointed as the programme secretariat and technical advisor to evaluate project proposals worth R153 billion across multiple sectors.

Our role involved comprehensive assessment of project feasibility, financial structuring, economic impact, and alignment with national development goals. We presented findings to various parliamentary committees and treasury officials to inform funding decisions.

Our Approach

We implemented a rigorous multi-criteria assessment framework that evaluated projects across several dimensions:

  • Economic viability and financial sustainability
  • Technical feasibility and implementation readiness
  • Social impact and job creation potential
  • Environmental sustainability considerations
  • Alignment with national development priorities

Our team conducted detailed due diligence on each project, including site visits, stakeholder consultations, and financial modeling to provide Treasury with independent, evidence-based recommendations.

Project Impact

  • Evaluated 47 major infrastructure projects with total value of R153 billion
  • Recommended funding for 32 projects that met strict viability criteria
  • Developed standardized assessment framework adopted by Treasury
  • Improved transparency in infrastructure funding decisions
  • Enhanced project preparation standards across government

Multi-purpose Reactor Project

Construction of 20-30MW nuclear reactor for medical radioisotope production and research

Client: Energy Department
Duration: 2020-2024
Location: Pelindaba, South Africa
Sector: Energy

Project Overview

The Multi-purpose Reactor (MPR) Project involves the construction of a 20-30MW nuclear reactor to replace South Africa's aging SAFARI-1 research reactor. The new facility will produce medical radioisotopes for both domestic use and export, while supporting nuclear research and development.

bd&company provided transaction advisory services including feasibility studies, financial modeling, and procurement strategy development. Our work helped structure this complex project to attract both public and private sector participation.

Technical Advisory

Our technical advisory services covered several critical areas:

  • Technology assessment and vendor selection process
  • Safety and regulatory compliance framework
  • Radioisotope production and commercialization strategy
  • Human capital development and skills transfer
  • Decommissioning and waste management planning

We worked closely with the Nuclear Energy Corporation of South Africa (NECSA) and international experts to ensure the project meets global best practices while addressing local requirements.

Project Gallery

Project Impact

  • Secured South Africa's position as global leader in medical radioisotope production
  • Will produce 25% of world's Molybdenum-99 supply for medical diagnostics
  • Creates 1,200 direct jobs during construction phase
  • Supports development of local nuclear expertise and supply chain
  • Expected to generate R3.5 billion annually in export revenue

Student Housing Infrastructure Programme

Development of student housing across PSET institutions across South Africa

Client: Education Department
Duration: 2019-2023
Location: 26 sites across South Africa
Sector: Education

Project Overview

The Student Housing Infrastructure Programme (SHIP) aims to address the critical shortage of student accommodation at Post-School Education and Training (PSET) institutions across South Africa. With a budget of R18 billion, the programme will deliver 300,000 new student beds through a combination of public funding and private sector participation.

bd&company served as the transaction advisor, developing the overall programme strategy, financial models, and procurement framework. We also provided capacity building to participating institutions to ensure successful implementation.

Innovative Approach

We developed an innovative delivery model that combines:

  • Direct delivery by institutions for smaller projects
  • PPP concessions for large-scale developments
  • Lease agreements with private providers
  • Partnerships with private developers

Our financial structuring ensured affordability for students while providing bankable projects for private investors. We also incorporated sustainable design principles and local economic development requirements into all projects.

Project Impact

  • Addresses critical shortage of 300,000 student beds nationwide
  • Creates 15,000 direct jobs during construction phase
  • Leverages R6 billion in private sector investment
  • Implements innovative affordability models for students
  • Incorporates green building standards across all developments

City of Johannesburg Waste-to-Energy

Project generating 28 MW from waste, recycling 35% of municipal waste

Client: City of Johannesburg
Duration: 2020-2024
Location: Johannesburg, South Africa
Sector: Energy

Project Overview

The City of Johannesburg Waste-to-Energy project is a pioneering facility that converts municipal solid waste into clean energy while maximizing recycling and minimizing landfill use. The plant processes 1,500 tons of waste daily, generating 28MW of electricity and recycling 35% of incoming materials.

bd&company served as the lead transaction advisor, structuring the PPP agreement, developing technical specifications, and managing the competitive bidding process.

Technical Features

  • Mass burn combustion technology with advanced emissions control
  • Materials recovery facility for recycling
  • Bottom ash processing for construction materials
  • District heating potential
  • Integrated education center

Our team developed innovative risk allocation mechanisms and performance guarantees to ensure long-term project success.
